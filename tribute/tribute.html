Prof. Wangari Maathai

Founder of the Green Belt Movement and the 2004 Nobel Peace Prize Laureate.

Here is a timeline of Prof. Wangari Maathai

  • 1940 - Born in Nyeri, Kenya.
  • 1964 - Obtained a degree in biological Sciences from Mount Saint Scholastica College in Atchison, Kansas.
  • 1966 - Obtained a Master of Science degree from the University of Pittsburgh.
  • 1971 - Pursued Doctoral Studies in Germany and the University of Nairobi, before obtaining a PhD from the University of Nairobi, where she taught Veterinary Anatomy.
  • 1976 - Became chair of the Department of Veterinary Anatomy.
  • 1977 - Became an associate Professor.
  • 1976-1987 - She was acive in the National Council of Women of Kenya.
  • 1981-1987 - Became the chairman at the National Council of Women of Kenya.
  • 2002-2007 - She represented the Tetu Constituency in the Kenyan Parliamnet.
  • 2002-2007 - Served as an Assistant Minister for Environment and Natural Resources in Kenya's 9th parlaiment.
  • 2005 - Appointed Goodwill Ambassador to the Congo Basin Forest Ecosystem.

  • 2009 - Named a UN messenger of Peace by the United Nations.
  • 2010 - Became a trstee of the Karura Forest Environmental Education Trust.
  • 2011 - Succumbed to ovarian cancer.
